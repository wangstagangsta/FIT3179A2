Australia’s Forest Fires (2016–21)

Australia’s forests experience significant fire activity each year — from planned prescribed burns to large-scale unplanned bushfires. The devastating 2019–20 “Black Summer” season alone burned over 8 million hectares of forest, destroying habitats and communities across the continent.

This dashboard visualises data from the ABARES “Fires in Australia’s forests 2016–21” dataset , showing both the percentage of forest area burned and the total area affected (hectares) by state and year.

Key takeaways

  • 2019–20 (shown as 2020) is the largest unplanned burn by area nationally.
